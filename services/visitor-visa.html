Visitor Visa

Australia is a highly popular destination for various purposes, including travel, business meetings, recreation, medical treatments, and more. To visit Australia, individuals need a valid visitor visa issued by the Australian authorities. At BM, our consultants assess your profile and provide support throughout the migration process, following the government's guidelines.

Australia's appeal as a destination stems from its diverse landscape, strong economy, beautiful cities like Sydney and Gold Coast, breathtaking wildlife, well-maintained road networks, high standard of living, and quality of life. These factors make Australia a desirable place to visit for people from around the world.

The Visitor Visa (subclass 600) is available for various purposes and has different streams:

  • Tourist stream: For individuals visiting Australia for holidays or recreation.

  • Business visitor stream: For individuals engaging in business activities or attending conferences.

  • Frequent traveler stream: For individuals who frequently travel to Australia for business or personal reasons.

  • Sponsored family stream: For individuals visiting family or relatives who are Australian citizens or permanent residents.

  • Approved destination status stream: For tourists from specific countries traveling in organised tour groups.

To be eligible for a visitor visa, individuals must have a clear purpose of visit, good health and moral conduct, economic stability with sufficient funds, sponsorship if applicable, intentions to return before the visa expires, and no outstanding debts to the Australian government.

The visitor visa allows visitors to enjoy privileges such as holidaying, conducting business, meeting family, short-term study for up to 3 months, or working as an unpaid volunteer. BM's experienced consultants guide applicants through the visa process, ensuring a hassle-free experience by providing assistance tailored to their needs and the specific visa streams.

In addition to the Visitor Visa (subclass 600), there are other visa options available for different purposes:

  • Medical Treatment Visa (subclass 602): For individuals seeking medical treatment or consultations in Australia.

  • Investor Retirement Visa (subclass 405): For self-funded retirees without dependents who wish to spend their retirement in Australia.

  • Special Program Visa (subclass 416): For participating in approved special programs or cultural exchanges, fostering international relations.

  • Transit Visa (subclass 771): For individuals and their dependent children transiting through Australia for up to 72 hours.

Boundless Migration extends its services beyond Australia to cater to those aiming to visit the USA, Canada, New Zealand, UK, and Schengen countries. Our expert team comprehends each individual's purpose of travel and offers tailored assistance in selecting the right visa category. We provide valuable guidance on fulfilling specific prerequisites, including health check-ups, financial documentation, character certifications, insurance, and accommodation arrangements. Your seamless and well-prepared journey is our priority.
